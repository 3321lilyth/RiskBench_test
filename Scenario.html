Scenario Taxonomy and Collection



Road Topology

We collect our scenario using CARLA smulator. The picture is Town03 in CRALA, which shows how we explicitly design road topology and label all erea in CARLA towns. The notations 'i', 't', 's', and 'r' indicate 4-way intersection, 3-way intersection, straight, and roundabout, respectively.

Table 1, Scenario Taxonomy, summarizes all possible values of each taxonomy attribute. Note that we further split the 3-way intersection into 3 categoriesbased on the junction the ego vehicle enters, and each category of 3-way intersection offers different afforded actions.

Scenario Types

We collect four types of scenario. Interactive scenarios are normally a negotiation situation. ego-vehicle is forced to yield to the others. we also collect collision and obstacle avoidance scenarios. In addition, we also collect non interactive scenarios, which has no risk in the scene.

Data Collection & Generation

  • Get the scenario script
    To collect a specific scenario, we propose a hierarchical scenario description as a script. The description is composed of static environment and dynamic elements. This figure illustrate an example: we choose a specific 4-way interaction, and ego-vehicle crossing the intersection and negotiating with a car trying to turn left.
  • Manually Collect the Scenario
    Once we have scenario description, we then manually collect the scenario data by following the script to play a scenario in the simlulator. In this way, we can obtain any specific safety-critical scenario.
  • Auto Augmentarion
    However, such collecting approach remains a great challenge of enormous manual effort. We thus propose a data generator to automatically augment the collected scenarios by changing the factor of time of day, weather, and traffic density. by doing this we can easily produce a large scale dataset for risk assessment benchmark.
